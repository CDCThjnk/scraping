Alexander Yevgenyevich Volkov(Russian:Александр Евгеньевич Волков;[1]born October 24, 1988) is a Russian professionalmixed martial artist. He currently competes in theHeavyweightdivision of theUltimate Fighting Championship(UFC). A professional since 2009, Volkov previously competed for theM-1 Globalpromotion, as well asBellator MMA. He is a former heavyweight champion in both promotions. As of June 24, 2025, he is #2 in theUFC heavyweight rankings.[4]

Mixed martial arts career

M-1 Global
In 2008 Volkov signed withM-1 Global. His professional MMA debut came in April 2009 when he defeated Nikolay Pleshakov byTKOin just 80 seconds.[5]Volkov followed this up a month later with another TKO victory, this time against Adam Alikhanov in just 20 seconds.[6]A further month later, Volkov suffered his first professional loss at the hands of Akhmed Sultanov. Midway through the first round, Volkov was forced to tap to an armbar. Volkov then went on a two-fight win streak, both via TKO.[6]
In December 2009, after just eight months as a professional fighter,[6]Volkov faced Ibragim Magomedov at M-1 Challenge 20 - 2009 Finals. Coming in as an underdog, his team simply wanted him to make it through all three rounds, but Volkov exceeded expectations by winning via unanimous decision.[7]Volkov was awarded an M-1 Global ring "as a reward for exceptional performance".[7]
Volkov faced Denis Smoldarev on February 19, 2016, M-1 Challenge 64 for the vacant M-1 Global Heavyweight title, he won the fight via submission (triangle choke).
Volkov faced former Bellator light heavyweight championAttila Véghon June 16, 2016, at M-1 Challenge 68. He won the fight via knockout in the first round. This and other fights of Alexander Volkov in the M-1 league can be viewed in theAll fights of Alexander Volkov in M-1 Globalof the M-1 Global TV channel.
Within his first year of professional competition, Volkov was competing in the M-1 Eastern European tournament,[7]taking victories in the opening round and the semi-final.[6]Volkov progressed to the M-1 Selection 2010 - Eastern Europe Finals, where a win would put him in line to challenge for the M-1 Global Heavyweight title at the2010 M-1 Challenge Seasonevents. To reach the championship fight, Volkov first had to take onMaxim Grishinto become the Eastern Europe champion. Unfortunately for Volkov, he fell prey to a rear-naked choke early in the opening round and was eliminated.[8]

Post M-1 Eastern European Tournament
Less than a month later, Volkov took his first fight outside of M-1 in nearly a year. His opponent was the debuting Eldar Yagudin, who he defeated inside the opening round by TKO (punches).[6]
On August 5, 2011, as part of League-70 Russia vs Brazil, held in Sochi, Russia, Alexander Volkov defeated the undefeated at that time Bulgarian Nedyalko Karadzhov via TKO in the opening round.

Bellator MMA
In September 2012, it was announced that Volkov would compete for Bellator Fighting Championships. He made his U.S. debut in theBellator season seven heavyweight tournament. In the opening, quarter-final round he facedBrett RogersatBellator 75. Volkov dominated the fight by making the most of his reach advantage. Volkov won via unanimous decision.[9]In the semifinals atBellator 80, he facedVinicius Queirozand won via TKO in the second round. On December 14, Volkov foughtRichard Halein a 5 round fight for Bellator Season 7 Heavyweight Tournament Final andBellator Heavyweight Championship. Volkov won via unanimous decision.[10]
In his first title defense, Volkov facedVitaly Minakovin the co-main event atBellator 108on November 15, 2013. He lost the bout and his title via TKO in the first round.[11]
Volkov facedMark Holataon March 7, 2014, atBellator 111in Heavyweight Tournament.[12]He won the fight via TKO in the first round.[13]Volkov facedMighty Moin the semifinals atBellator 116on April 11, 2014.[14]He won the fight via spectacular knockout in the first round.[15]
Volkov facedBlagoy Ivanovin the finals on May 17, 2014, atBellator 120. He won the fight via submission in the second round.[16]
Nearly a year since his last fight for the promotion, Volkov facedTony JohnsonatBellator 136on April 10, 2015.[17]He lost the fight via split decision.
Volkov facedCheick KongoatBellator 139on June 26, 2015.[18]He lost the fight via unanimous decision marking his second consecutive loss being cut by the Bellator MMA.[19]

Ultimate Fighting Championship
In September 2016, it was announced that Volkov has signed with theUltimate Fighting Championship. He made his debut againstTimothy Johnsonon November 19, 2016, atUFC Fight Night 99.[20]Volkov was awarded a controversial split decision victory.[21]12 out of 12 media scores gave the bout to Johnson.[22]
Volkov facedRoy Nelsonon April 15, 2017, atUFC on Fox 24.[23]He won the fight via unanimous decision.[24]
Volkov facedStefan Struveon September 2, 2017, atUFC Fight Night: Volkov vs. Struve.[25]He won the bout via TKO in the third round.[26]The win also earned Volkov his firstFight of the Nightbonus award.[27]
Volkov facedFabrício Werdumon March 17, 2018, atUFC Fight Night 127.[28]He won the back-and-forth fight via knockout in the fourth round.[29]The win also earned Volkov his firstPerformance of the Nightbonus award.[30]
Volkov weighed in atUFC 226on July 6, 2018, serving as the backup for the heavyweight title bout betweenStipe MiocicandDaniel Cormier.[31]
Volkov facedDerrick Lewison October 6, 2018, atUFC 229.[32]Volkov dominated the fight, hurting Lewis multiple times, but ultimately lost the fight via knockout late in round three.[33]
Volkov was expected to faceAlistair Overeemon April 20, 2019, atUFC Fight Night 149.[34]However, on April 3, 2019, it was reported Volkov pulled out from the fight for undisclosed reason.[35]
Volkov was scheduled to faceJunior Dos Santoson November 9, 2019, atUFC on ESPN+ 21.[36]However, dos Santos was forced to pull out from the event due to contracting a serious bacterial infection,[37]and he was replaced byGreg Hardy.[38]He won the fight via unanimous decision.[39]
Volkov facedCurtis Blaydeson June 20, 2020, atUFC on ESPN: Blaydes vs. Volkov.[40]Volkov lost the fight by unanimous decision.[41]
Volkov facedWalt Harrison October 24, 2020 atUFC 254.[42]He won the fight via technical knockout in round two.[43]
Volkov facedAlistair Overeemon February 6, 2021, atUFC Fight Night 184.[44]He won the fight via technical knockout in round two.[45]This win earned him thePerformance of the Nightaward.[46]
Volkov facedCiryl Ganeon June 26, 2021, atUFC Fight Night 190.[47]He lost the fight via unanimous decision.[48]
Volkov facedMarcin Tyburaon October 30, 2021, atUFC 267.[49]He won the bout via unanimous decision.[50]
Volkov facedTom Aspinallon March 19, 2022,  atUFC Fight Night 204.[51]Volkov lost the fight via straight armbar submission in round one.[52]
Volkov facedJairzinho Rozenstruikon June 4, 2022, atUFC Fight Night 207.[53]He won the fight via technical knockout in round one.[54]
Volkov facedAlexander Romanovon March 11, 2023, atUFC Fight Night 221.[55]He won the fight via technical knockout in the first round.[56]
Volkov facedTai Tuivasaon September 10, 2023, atUFC 293.[57]He won the fight with Ezekiel Choke in the second round.[58]With this win, Volkov became the second person in UFC history to finish a fight with an Ezekiel Choke.[59]
Volkov facedSergei Pavlovichon June 22, 2024, atUFC on ABC 6.[60]He won the fight by unanimous decision.[61]
Volkov was scheduled to face former interimUFC Heavyweight ChampionCiryl Ganein a rematch on October 26, 2024 atUFC 308.[62]However, due to Volkov's knee injury,[63]the bout was moved and took place on December 7, 2024 atUFC 310.[64]Volkov lost the close fight by a controversial, split decision.[65]19 out of 20 media outlets scored the bout for Volkov.[66]UFC CEO Dana White apologized to Volkov after the bout saying he thought Volkov had won the bout and the head of the commission explained to White what was thought to be the reasoning of the judges' scorecards.[67][68]Volkov voiced his frustration about one of the judges, Adalaide Byrd, who has had a history of questionable scorecards in both MMA and boxing.[69]
Volkov is scheduled to faceJailton Almeidaon October 25, 2025, atUFC 321.[70]

Fighting style
Volkov is primarily a standup striker. He utilizes his considerable height, long reach, and powerful kicks to deliver accurate strikes from a distance.[71]In particular, he is known for his patient and technicalboxingas well as his punishing body kicks, which he utilized atUFC 254in his knockout victory againstWalt Harris.[72]
